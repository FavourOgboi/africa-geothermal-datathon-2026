# Methodology Notes

## Geothermal Screening Approach

The screening methodology prioritises **transmissivity** over temperature because:

1. Medium-temperature geothermal systems (60-90°C) depend critically on sustainable flow rates
2. Heat pumps can upgrade moderate temperatures efficiently, but cannot compensate for poor hydraulic productivity
3. PKP-01 demonstrates this: highest temperature (88°C) but near-zero transmissivity (0.1 Dm) renders it non-viable

## Multi-Criteria Ranking Weights

| Criterion | Weight | Justification |
|-----------|--------|--------------|
| Transmissivity | 0.30 | Primary hydraulic viability indicator |
| Flow Rate | 0.25 | Direct thermal extraction capacity |
| Power | 0.20 | Combined thermal output potential |
| Temperature | 0.10 | Heat quality (upgradeable via heat pump) |
| Permeability | 0.10 | Matrix flow quality |
| Thickness | 0.05 | Reservoir volume indicator |

## Thermal Power Calculation

Q = ṁ × Cp × ΔT

Where:
- ṁ = mass flow rate (kg/s) = volumetric flow rate × brine density
- Cp = 4250 J/kg·K (brine specific heat capacity)
- ΔT = production temperature − reinjection temperature
- Brine density ρ = 1078 kg/m³

## ATES Design Philosophy

- Warm well: 25–35°C (stores excess geothermal heat)
- Cold well: 8–15°C (stores winter cold for summer cooling)
- Reduces heat pump dependency during peak demand
- Enables seasonal thermal balancing
